Standard Property and Value
You may use familiar utility classes for standard values that include any characters except for space or hyphen.
<div know="color-000 font-size-18px light">black 18px light</div>
<div know="color-f00 font-size-24px bold">red 24px bold</div>
<div know="color-00f font-size-18px semibold">blue 14px semibold</div>
Complex Property and Value
Values with a space or hyphen should use an equal sign between the property and value to indicate which is which, with spaces converted to forward slash to keep the multi-value together in one property.

Note: box-sizing-border-box would be interpeted as box-sizing-border: box; instead of the intended box-sizing: border-box;, so an = helps in this instance.
<div know="box-sizing=border-box border=1px/solid/red padding=5px/10px">red border with padding</div>

<div know="box-sizing=border-box border=2px/solid/blue margin=5px/10px">blue border with margin</div>
!Important Modifier
The !important modifier will apply if ! appears anywhere in the value or if -important appears at the end of a value.
<div know="font-size-24px!">force 24px font-size</div>
<div know="font-size-12px-important!">force 10px font-size</div>
Multiple values may be grouped to apply !important to each with the !{} or important{} selector.
<div know="!{color-c33 padding-2px}">force red with padding</div>
<div know="important{color-3c3 font-size-12px}">force blue 12px font-size</div>
Colors and Shading
3 or 6 digit hex colors or explicit common color names may be used with or without the leading # character.
<div know="color-c00 border=1px/solid/#c00">red</div>
<div know="color-00cc00 border=1px/solid/#00cc00">green</div>
<div know="color-#00c border=1px/solid/#00c">blue</div>
<div know="color-#cccc00 border=1px/solid/#cccc00">yellow</div>
<div know="color-orange border=1px/solid/orange">orange</div>
Any hex color may be shaded at 0-100 percent with @ giving you full access to all 16,777,216 possible colors.
<div know="color-f00@100">#ff0000</div>
<div know="color-f00@95">#f30000</div>
<div know="color-f00@90">#e60000</div>
<div know="color-f00@85">#d90000</div>
<div know="color-f00@80">#cc0000</div>
<div know="color-f00@70">#800000</div>
<div know="color-f00@60">#990000</div>
<div know="color-f00@50">#800000</div>
<div know="color-f00@40">#660000</div>
<div know="color-f00@30">#4d0000</div>
<div know="color-f00@0">#000000</div>
Any hex color may be converted to rgba() with ~ giving you access to opaque elements.
<div know="background-color-f00~100">#ff0000</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~90">rgba(255,0,0,0.9);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~80">rgba(255,0,0,0.8);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~70">rgba(255,0,0,0.7);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~60">rgba(255,0,0,0.6);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~50">rgba(255,0,0,0.5);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~40">rgba(255,0,0,0.4);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~30">rgba(255,0,0,0.3);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~20">rgba(255,0,0,0.2);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~10">rgba(255,0,0,0.1);</div>
<div know="background-color-f00~0">rgba(255,0,0,0);</div>
Both can be combined to create complex rgba() values with shades and opaque elements.
<div know="color-f00@0~100">#000</div>
<div know="color-f00@10~90">rgba(26,0,0,0.9);</div>
<div know="color-f00@20~80">rgba(51,0,0,0.8);</div>
<div know="color-f00@30~70">rgba(77,0,0,0.7);</div>
<div know="color-f00@40~60">rgba(102,0,0,0.6);</div>
<div know="color-f00@50~50">rgba(128,0,0,0.5);</div>
